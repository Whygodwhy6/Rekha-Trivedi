Indian Classical Singer

About: Rekha Trivedi is an Indian singer known for her contributions to Gujarati and Rajasthani music. From a young age, she displayed a strong inclination towards music, which her parents nurtured. She received classical training under Shri Rasiklal Andharia in Bhavnagar, Gujarat, and later earned a degree in music (Sangeet Visharad) from Gandharva Sangeet Vidyalaya in Mumbai. Her career gained momentum when renowned Gujarati composer Shri Avinash Vyas selected her for his program "Alakh Ne Otle," where she captivated audiences with the song "Ram Tame Sitajini Tole Na Avo." Rekha has collaborated with esteemed music directors such as Ninu Mazumdar, Dilip Dholakia, and Ashit Desai. She is a regular broadcaster on All India Radio and Doordarshan and has performed internationally in countries like Belgium and Singapore. Her recordings are available under labels like T-Series and Venus, and she has lent her voice to various Hindi and Rajasthani albums, showcasing her versatility across languages.
